Here are some key commercial case acts that are significant in the legal landscape:

1. **The Commercial Courts Act, 2015**:
   - **Objective**: This act was enacted to streamline and expedite the resolution of commercial disputes in India. It aims to improve the efficiency and reduce delays in the disposal of commercial cases³.
   - **Key Features**:
     - Establishment of Commercial Courts, Commercial Divisions, and Commercial Appellate Divisions in High Courts.
     - Summary judgment provisions to decide claims based on documentary evidence without recording oral evidence².
     - Case management system to ensure timely resolution of disputes².

2. **The Companies Act, 2013**:
   - **Objective**: This act regulates the incorporation, responsibilities, and dissolution of companies in India.
   - **Key Features**:
     - Provisions for corporate governance, mergers and acquisitions, and the protection of shareholders' rights.
     - Establishment of the National Company Law Tribunal (NCLT) and the National Company Law Appellate Tribunal (NCLAT) for adjudicating disputes related to companies.

3. **The Insolvency and Bankruptcy Code, 2016**:
   - **Objective**: This code consolidates and amends the laws relating to reorganization and insolvency resolution of corporate persons, partnership firms, and individuals.
   - **Key Features**:
     - Time-bound process for resolving insolvency.
     - Establishment of the Insolvency and Bankruptcy Board of India (IBBI) to oversee the implementation of the code.
     - Provisions for the insolvency resolution process for individuals and partnership firms.




Here are some key sections of the IPC that are often relevant in commercial cases:

1. **Section 405 - Criminal Breach of Trust**:
   - This section deals with situations where a person entrusted with property or dominion over property dishonestly misappropriates or converts it to their own use.

2. **Section 415 - Cheating**:
   - This section defines cheating and includes acts where a person deceives another to deliver any property or valuable security.

3. **Section 420 - Cheating and Dishonestly Inducing Delivery of Property**:
   - This section is specifically about cheating that leads to the delivery of property, or the making, alteration, or destruction of a valuable security.

4. **Section 463 - Forgery**:
   - This section deals with the making of a false document with the intent to cause damage or injury.

5. **Section 468 - Forgery for Purpose of Cheating**:
   - This section addresses forgery committed with the intent to cheat.

6. **Section 471 - Using as Genuine a Forged Document**:
   - This section deals with using a forged document as if it were genuine.
